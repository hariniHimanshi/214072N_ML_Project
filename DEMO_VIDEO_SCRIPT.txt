[00:00 - 00:25]  OPENING — What the App Does
================================================================================

ACTION: Screen shows the full Streamlit app on the Prediction tab.
        The gauge should be visible with a default probability.

SAY:
  "Hi, this is my Machine Learning assignment demo for the Colombo
   Heavy Rain Prediction project.

   The goal of this project is to predict whether the NEXT week in
   Colombo, Sri Lanka, will experience heavy rainfall — meaning more than
   15 millimetres of total precipitation — using 18 years of historical
   weather data from NOAA.

   I built an XGBoost Classifier and wrapped it in this interactive
   Streamlit web application, which I'll walk you through now."


================================================================================
[00:25 - 01:00]  SIDEBAR — Explaining the Inputs
================================================================================

ACTION: Point to / hover over the sidebar sliders one by one.

SAY:
  "On the left side, we have the input panel. This is where you enter
   the CURRENT week's observed weather data.

   You can adjust:
   - The week of the year — which captures the seasonal monsoon pattern,
   - The average, max, and min temperatures for this week,
   - The total precipitation that has already fallen this week,
   - And how many days it rained.

   Below that, we have the LAG features — last week's and two weeks ago's
   rainfall and temperature. These are crucial because rainfall in Colombo
   tends to persist — a wet week is often followed by another wet week,
   especially during the monsoon seasons.

   All of these 14 features are fed into the trained XGBoost model."


================================================================================
[01:00 - 01:45]  PREDICTION — Dry Week Scenario
================================================================================

ACTION: Set sliders to a DRY scenario:
  - Week of Year      : 5   (early February — dry season)
  - Avg Temp          : 29.0
  - Max Temp          : 33.0
  - Min Temp          : 25.0
  - This week's PRCP  : 2.0 mm
  - Rainy days        : 1
  - Last week PRCP    : 3.0 mm
  - Two weeks ago PRCP: 1.5 mm
  - Last week Avg Temp: 29.0
  - Two weeks ago Temp: 28.5
  - Rainy days last wk: 1

  The gauge should show LOW probability (green zone).

SAY:
  "Let me start with a typical DRY season scenario — early February,
   which falls between Sri Lanka's two monsoon periods.

   I've set this week's rainfall to just 2mm, with only 1 rainy day,
   and low rainfall in the previous two weeks as well.

   You can see the gauge is pointing well below 50%, showing the model
   predicts a DRY week ahead — with low rain probability.

   The verdict at the bottom confirms: DRY Week.

   Now let's look at the SHAP explanation on the right — this is the
   really interesting part..."


================================================================================
[01:45 - 02:30]  SHAP WATERFALL — Explaining the Prediction
================================================================================

ACTION: Point to the SHAP waterfall chart on the right side of the screen.

SAY:
  "The bar chart here is called a SHAP Waterfall — it shows exactly
   WHY the model made this prediction.

   The BLUE bars are features pushing the prediction TOWARD a dry week.
   The RED bars push it TOWARD a rainy week.

   In this case, you can see that features like low lag1_prcp — meaning
   little rain last week — and the timing in week 5 — which is the dry
   season — are the dominant factors pushing this firmly into
   the 'Dry' category.

   This is what makes SHAP so powerful for explainability. We are not
   just getting a black-box answer — we can see exactly which inputs
   drove the model's decision, and in which direction."


================================================================================
[02:30 - 03:05]  PREDICTION — Rainy Week Scenario
================================================================================

  - Week of Year      : 42  (mid-October — peak monsoon)
  - Avg Temp          : 27.0
  - Max Temp          : 29.5
  - Min Temp          : 24.0
  - This week's PRCP  : 85.0 mm
  - Rainy days        : 6
  - Last week PRCP    : 60.0 mm
  - Two weeks ago PRCP: 45.0 mm
  - Last week Avg Temp: 27.0
  - Two weeks ago Temp: 27.5
  - Rainy days last wk: 5

  The gauge should swing into HIGH probability (blue zone).

SAY:
  "Now let me simulate the peak of Sri Lanka's northeast monsoon
   — week 42, which is mid-October.

   I've set this week's total rainfall to 85mm, with 6 out of 7 days
   being rainy, and similarly heavy rainfall in the past two weeks.

   Watch the gauge — it swings all the way to the right, showing a HIGH
   probability of heavy rain next week as well.

   The model predicts: Heavy Rain Week.

   And looking at the SHAP chart, the story is now completely reversed —
   high lag1_prcp and prcp_sum are now the dominant RED features,
   strongly pushing the prediction toward a rainy outcome.

   The model has clearly learned the persistence of Sri Lanka's
   monsoon rainfall patterns."
